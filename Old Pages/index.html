What Counts as Hours? How Can You Count Them?

How Can I Get Hours?

In order to be counted, all outreach hours must be submitted as you complete them, within 7 days
of the end of the event, to our outreach website. If legitimate, your hours will be approved by
the Outreach Vice President and a mentor of Mentor Leadership.

The following volunteering events, which are subject to change, are those we attend year-round.
Listed next to each event is its approximate month, the potential number of hours you can earn
for your volunteering, and the approximate number of students who will be chosen to volunteer.
Please keep in mind that each event may span more than one day.

  1. Feed my Starving Children (January, 3 hours, 7 students)
  2. ARIS STEM Summit (February, 4 hours, 11 students)
  3. Triangle School Science Fair (February, 3 hours, 15 students)
  4. Woods Road School Science Fair (March, 2 hours, 10 students)
  5. HES Science Fair (March, 2 hours, 10 students)
  6. Amsterdam School Science Fair (April, 3 hours, 10 students)
  7. Relay for Life (April, 3+ hours, 7 students)
  8. Take Your Child To Work Day (April, 7 hours, 8 students)
  9. Exploration Fair (June, 4 hours, 20 students)
  10. Summer Enrichment (June; over 10 days, 3 hours/day, 10 students)
  11. 4H Fair (August; over 3 days, 3 hours/day, 8 students/day)
  12. Rotary Fair (August; over 5 days, 5 hours/day, 10 students/day)
  13. Steeplechase Run (September, 5 hours, 5 students)
  14. Barnes and Noble (Nov-Dec; over 4 events, 5 hours/event, 3 students)

This list contains the number of hours you can earn for outreach that is non-event volunteering.
Each hour count next to the outreach is the exact number of hours you will earn for completing
the task (no more, no less). Your work must be placed into the respective folder in the team
drive for Executive Leadership to approve.

  1. Virtual mentoring writing one script (1 hour)
  2. Virtual mentoring filming one video (1.5 hours)
  3. WISE-in-a-Box video writing one script (1 hour)
  4. WISE-in-a-Box filming one video (1.5 hours)
  5. BoroBlast referee kit building (5 hours)
  6. Mentoring a FIRST team (may vary; hours must be approved by the Team Coach)
  7. Other outreach planning (may vary; hours will be set by the lead and Outreach Vice President)
  8. BoroBlast referee attending FLL events for training (may vary; hours must be approved by Adult Lead of BoroBlast Referees)

How Can I Count My Hours?

In order to record your hours, each member will have to fill out the designated attendance sheet for a volunteering event.
