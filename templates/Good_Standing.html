Behavior

Section 4.2: Behavior: A student’s behavior must be compliant with the Hillsborough High School handbook. A student may not have a record of poor behavior requiring disciplinary action (either on the school or on the team). If a student repeatedly displays bad behavior, it is up to the discretion of the Faculty Advisor to put him/her on Behavioral Probation. Members on probation or suspension for behavioral issues are not allowed to attend outreach events or recognition events (e.g. receiving proclamations, being recognized by the Board of Education, etc.).
Section 9.1.A: 1. To be eligible for Robotics during the first semester of grade 10, 11 or 12, a pupil must pass 30 credits during the preceding academic year.
2. To be eligible for Robotics competition during the second semester of grade 9, 10, 11, or 12, a pupil must pass 15 credits during the first semester. Full year courses are equated as one-half the total credit value of the course for the year.
a. Students must earn at least a 75 average in every subject each semester. Students failing to achieve a final average of 75 or better in two or more subjects will be declared ineligible for the next semester. Students who fail to achieve a final average of 75 or better in one subject shall be placed on probation for the next marking period.
b. Probationary students’ grades will be monitored during the marking period. A probationary student who becomes ineligible for failing to maintain a semester average of 75 or better shall be required to maintain an average of 75 or better in all subjects for one marking period before returning to the robotics program.
c. Students will strive to maintain a minimum grade average of 75 in every course. If a grade in any subject is lower than 75, a progress report will be requested of the teacher. If improvement is not reported after a three-week time period, possible suspension from the team will occur after consultation with the adviser, guidance counselor, and administration. If the students grade falls below 70, the student will be suspended from the robotics team until the grade is elevated.
Section 10.1.B.1: Student Participation: Students who do not hold a leadership position must have a meeting attendance rate of 80% (when excused absences are excluded) and 70% (when excused absences are included) throughout the school year until the new leadership takes effect. From when new leadership takes effect until the start of the school year, students must have a meeting attendance rate of 70% (when excused absences are excluded) and 60% (when excused absences are included).
Section 1.1.B.2: Participation of all students in elected/appointed positions: Students who hold any elected or appointed position must have a meeting attendance rate of 85% including excused absences. From when new leadership takes effect until the start of the school year, students must have a meeting attendance rate of 75% (when excused absences are included).
Section 1.1.C: Outreach Requirement: All RoboRaiders should be active members of outreach initiatives. In order to be an active member, a returning RoboRaider must participate for a minimum of 25 hours of nonmandatory outreach, throughout the school year and summer, by one month prior to the start of Build Season. A new RoboRaider, must participate for a minimum number of hours calculated by: # of months between joining the team to one month prior to the start of Build Season *2. This hour requirement can be fulfilled anytime throughout the time period indicated (this just pertains to freshmen, sophomores, and juniors). However, seniors must complete at least 10 hours from one month before Kickoff till the new leadership takes effect. This outreach requirement also includes active involvement in planning or implementing an outreach program, in addition to in-person volunteering. Members who play key roles during Build Season can be excused from fulfilling the outreach requirement if they are needed in high-priority team responsibilities. Key roles include, but are not limited to, Drive Team, Chairman’s Presenters, and those working directly on the robot outside warehouse hours. The President, Mentor Leadership, and Faculty Advisors must approve of all members seeking an excused status. However, excused members must fulfill an average of two hours per month excluding Build Season.
Section 1.1.D: Productivity at Meetings Expectation: All RoboRaiders are expected to participate productively at team meetings. Students who do not hold leadership positions must maintain an average of at least an 85% on their completed deliverables. Students who hold leadership positions must maintain an average of at least a 90% on their completed deliverables. Subteam managers who receive 3 warnings are eligible for dismissal.
Section 4.4.A: Team Dues: There are large costs associated with designing and building a robot and not all of these costs can be absorbed by our generous sponsors. Subsequently, every member of Team 75 is required to pay a due by Kickoff, the amount of which will be specified in September.
